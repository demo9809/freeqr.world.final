Is a WiFi QR Code Secure?

A WiFi QR code is as secure as giving someone your password verbally or written on paper — it's a convenient way to share access you've chosen to share. Your password is encoded inside the QR image. Anyone who scans it can connect to your network, so only share it with people you trust.

FreeQR specifically is secure in its generation process: all QR code creation happens locally in your browser using JavaScript. Your WiFi password is never transmitted to FreeQR servers, never stored, and never logged.

  • Change your WiFi password if you think the QR code has been shared unintentionally
  • Use WPA2 or WPA3 security (avoid WEP — it's outdated and easily cracked)
  • Consider creating a separate "guest network" if you want to limit guest access

WiFi QR Code — Frequently Asked Questions

How does a WiFi QR code work?
A WiFi QR code encodes your network credentials using the standard WIFI: URI scheme (e.g. WIFI:T:WPA;S:MyNetwork;P:MyPassword;;). When a smartphone camera scans this code, the operating system (iOS or Android) detects the WIFI format and automatically prompts the user to join that network — no typing required.
Does a WiFi QR code work on iPhone?
Yes. iPhone cameras (iOS 11 and later) can scan WiFi QR codes natively. Open the Camera app, point it at the QR code, and tap the notification to connect. No third-party app needed.
Does a WiFi QR code work on Android?
Yes. Android phones with Google Lens or the native camera app (Android 9+) scan WiFi QR codes natively. Some older Android devices may need a QR code scanner app.
What security types are supported?
FreeQR supports WPA/WPA2 (the most common for home and business networks), WPA3 (newer standard), WEP (legacy), and open networks with no password.
Is my WiFi password safe if I use FreeQR?
Yes. FreeQR generates QR codes entirely in your browser using JavaScript. Your WiFi password is never sent to any server, never stored in any database, and never logged. The QR code is created locally on your device.
Can I download the WiFi QR code as SVG for printing?
Yes. FreeQR lets you download your WiFi QR code as SVG (infinitely scalable, best for print), PNG (up to 4K), JPG, or PDF. SVG is recommended for large-format printing like restaurant signage or framed prints.
