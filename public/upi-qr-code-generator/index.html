How Does a UPI QR Code Work?

FreeQR generates a standard UPI deep link (upi://pay?pa=[upi-id]&pn=[name]&am=[amount]&cu=INR) and encodes it into a QR code. When a customer scans it with any UPI-enabled app, the app reads the deep link, pre-fills your UPI ID and the amount, and shows the customer a payment confirmation screen. They approve with their UPI PIN and the payment arrives in your bank account within seconds.

The QR code follows the NPCI (National Payments Corporation of India) UPI standard, which means it is compatible with every bank and payments app that is certified for UPI in India. No third-party middleman, no extra transaction fee to FreeQR — the payment goes directly from the customer's bank to yours through the UPI network.

UPI QR Code — Frequently Asked Questions

What is a UPI QR code?
A UPI QR code encodes your UPI ID (Unified Payments Interface identifier, such as yourname@okaxis) and optional payment details using the standard NPCI UPI deep link format. When scanned with any UPI app — Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM — it opens the payment screen with your ID pre-filled so the customer can pay immediately.
Is the UPI QR code generator free?
Yes — completely free. No account, no email required, no watermark on the downloaded QR code, and no usage limits. FreeQR generates your UPI QR code entirely in your browser. Your UPI ID is never sent to any FreeQR server.
Does the UPI QR code work with Google Pay, PhonePe and Paytm?
Yes. FreeQR generates a standard NPCI UPI deep link QR code that is supported by all UPI-certified applications, including Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM, Amazon Pay, iMobile Pay, and virtually every Indian banking app that supports UPI payments.
Can I set a fixed amount in the UPI QR code?
Yes. FreeQR lets you enter a fixed payment amount in Indian Rupees. When someone scans the code, that amount is pre-filled in their UPI app. This is ideal for fixed-price products, delivery fees, or service charges. Leave the amount field blank if you want the payer to enter any amount themselves.
Is it safe to display a UPI QR code publicly?
Yes. Your UPI QR code encodes your UPI ID, which is safe to share publicly — just like sharing your bank account number. Anyone can send money to it, but no one can withdraw money from your account using just a UPI ID. Always check your printed QR code daily to ensure no one has covered it with a tampered sticker — this is a known offline fraud technique.
