vCard QR Code — Frequently Asked Questions

What is a vCard QR code?
A vCard QR code encodes your contact information — name, phone, email, company, address, website — in the standard vCard 3.0 format. When someone scans it with their smartphone camera, it instantly prompts them to save you as a new contact. No typing, no errors, no app required beyond the built-in camera.
Is the vCard QR code generator free?
Yes — completely free. No account, no email address required, no watermark on the downloaded QR code, and no download limit. FreeQR generates your vCard QR code entirely in your browser with no data ever sent to a server.
Does a vCard QR code work on iPhone and Android?
Yes. iPhone (iOS 11+) and Android devices with a modern camera app or Google Lens can scan vCard QR codes natively. The operating system reads the vCard data and offers to save it directly to the address book — no third-party scanner app needed.
Can I update my vCard QR code after printing?
A standard static vCard QR code encodes your contact details directly in the QR pattern. If you change your phone number or email, you will need to generate a new QR code and reprint it. For business cards already in circulation, consider using a URL QR code that redirects to a hosted contact page you can update at any time.
What contact fields can I include in a vCard QR code?
FreeQR supports: first and last name, job title, organisation/company, mobile phone, work phone, email address, website URL, and physical address (street, city, country). You can fill in as many or as few fields as you need — only the fields you complete will be included in the QR code.
