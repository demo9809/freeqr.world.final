Does a Logo in the Center Affect Scanning?

This is the most common question about logo QR codes — and the answer is no, as long as the logo is sized correctly. QR codes are engineered with a built-in error correction system. At the highest correction level (Level H), a QR code can still scan successfully even if up to 30% of its pattern is covered or damaged.

FreeQR automatically switches to Level H error correction whenever a center logo is detected. The logo size slider is constrained to keep the logo within the safe zone. The result is a QR code that looks great and scans reliably — on print materials, screens, packaging, and even slightly worn surfaces.

  • Always test-scan your branded QR code on both iPhone and Android before printing at scale
  • Avoid placing logos that are too complex or detailed — simple, high-contrast logos work best
  • Use PNG with transparent background to avoid a white square box around the logo
  • High-contrast QR colors (dark on light) improve scan speed and reliability

QR Code with Logo — Frequently Asked Questions

Does adding a logo to a QR code affect scannability?
No — not when the logo is properly sized. QR codes include built-in error correction that allows up to 30% of the pattern to be obscured. FreeQR automatically uses the maximum error correction level (H) when a logo is present and constrains the logo to the safe coverage zone, ensuring reliable scanning even with a large logo in the center.
What logo file formats does FreeQR accept?
FreeQR accepts PNG, JPG, JPEG, WEBP and SVG logo files. PNG with a transparent background is strongly recommended — the transparent areas blend seamlessly with your chosen QR background color, avoiding the white rectangle that appears with JPG logos.

What size should my logo be for the best result?
Upload a square or near-square logo at a minimum of 200×200 pixels. Larger is better — FreeQR will scale it down. PNG format with a transparent background produces the cleanest result. Avoid very complex logos with thin lines or fine text — these become illegible at the small size of a QR center logo.
